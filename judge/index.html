Seeking judicial seat, willing to relocate

The state Supreme Court put longtime Lake County resident James L. Kaplan on the Cook County bench in 2010, and gave him a second appointment in 2012. Kaplan has rented two different condos in those subcircuits while his family remains in their Lincolnshire home. A continuing joint probe by Medill Watchdog at Northwestern University and WGN Investigates reveals how Judge Kaplan jumped from subcircuit to subcircuit to hold onto a spot on the bench.

James L. Kaplan's family has for decades lived in their home in Lincolnshire, Lake County.

After Governor Blagojevich appointed Kaplan to the Illinois Court of Claims in 2005, he was able to stay with his family in their Lake County home.

When the state Supreme Court prepared to appoint him in 2010 to a vacant judicial seat in Cook County's 8th subcircuit, Kaplan rented a condominium in Chicago's River North neighborhood.

He took the bench, but lost the March 2012 Democratic primary to fill that seat. His appointment remained effective until Dec. 3, 2012.

After he lost the primary to fill that 8th subcircuit seat, the state Supreme Court gave him another appointment: This one, an appointment to a vacancy in the 12th subcircuit, covering Cook County's North Shore suburbs.

Kaplan rented another condominium — this time, in Glenview — and now is a candidate to hold onto his seat in the coming March Democratic primary.
